## How It Works

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Discovery & Data Collection (Weeks 1-2)
- Ingest data from CMDB, financial systems, APM tools, and service desk
- Establish complete application inventory with metadata
- Map costs, usage patterns, and integrations
- Identify data gaps and enrichment needs

**Client Effort**: Provide data access, identify data owners
**Our Effort**: Data extraction, cleansing, normalization

#### Step 2: Stakeholder Assessment (Weeks 2-4)
- Conduct 30-45 minute structured interviews with business and technical stakeholders
- Capture qualitative insights on business value, user satisfaction, technical health
- Gather forward-looking needs and strategic alignment perspectives
- Score responses across 7 assessment categories

**Client Effort**: Make stakeholders available for interviews
**Our Effort**: Schedule, conduct, and compile all assessments

#### Step 3: Analysis & Scoring (Weeks 4-5)
- Apply weighted scoring model combining quantitative and qualitative data
- Run applications through TIME disposition framework
- Perform cluster analysis to identify consolidation opportunities
- Calculate potential savings and build preliminary business case

**Client Effort**: Validate assumptions and preliminary findings
**Our Effort**: All analysis, scoring, and modeling

#### Step 4: Recommendations & Roadmap (Weeks 5-7)
- Develop detailed recommendations for each disposition category
- Create sequenced implementation roadmap
- Identify quick wins for immediate action
- Build executive-ready business case with ROI projections

**Client Effort**: Review and provide feedback on recommendations
**Our Effort**: Recommendation development, roadmap creation, business case

#### Step 5: Delivery & Knowledge Transfer (Weeks 7-8)
- Present findings to executive leadership
- Train client team on rationalization tool
- Document processes for ongoing portfolio management
- Hand off all data and materials

**Client Effort**: Attend presentations, participate in training
**Our Effort**: Presentations, training, documentation, handoff

## Tough Questions & Strong Answers

### 1. "We already know what we have"

**Answer**: "Most of our clients thought the same thing until they discovered their CMDB had only 60-70% of actual applications. We consistently find shadow IT, departmental purchases, and legacy systems that never made it into official inventories. Our discovery process typically identifies 30-40% more applications than clients expected."

### 2. "We tried this before and it failed"

**Answer**: "Traditional rationalization projects fail for two reasons: they're too slow (18+ months) and they lack stakeholder buy-in. We deliver results in 8-10 weeks by combining automated data collection with targeted stakeholder assessments. We don't just tell you what to do—we capture why from the people who use these systems every day."

### 3. "We don't have budget for this"

**Answer**: "This engagement pays for itself within the first month of implementation. A typical mid-size engagement costs $100-150K and identifies $3-5M in annual savings. The question isn't whether you can afford to do this—it's whether you can afford not to."

### 4. "Our stakeholders won't have time for interviews"

**Answer**: "We've designed our assessment to take just 30-45 minutes per stakeholder. For a typical engagement, we need 30-40 interviews over 2-3 weeks. That's less than 1% of your stakeholders' time to deliver 15-25% cost savings. We handle all scheduling and can do interviews via video call at their convenience."

### 5. "Every application is critical to someone"

**Answer**: "That's exactly why we use a multi-dimensional scoring model rather than asking 'is this important?' We assess business criticality, but also user satisfaction, technical health, strategic alignment, and cost efficiency. This reveals applications that feel important but deliver low value relative to their cost and risk."

### 6. "We're in the middle of a cloud migration"

**Answer**: "Perfect timing. You need to know what's worth migrating before you migrate it. 20-30% of applications in a typical portfolio should be retired, not migrated. We see clients waste millions lifting-and-shifting apps that should have been eliminated. Rationalize first, then migrate."

### 7. "How do we know your recommendations will work?"

**Answer**: "Every recommendation is backed by data from your environment and validated with your stakeholders. We don't prescribe solutions from outside—we synthesize insights from your own people and systems. During validation sessions, we review all findings with IT and business leadership before finalizing."

### 8. "What about the political challenges?"

**Answer**: "The biggest barrier to rationalization is usually internal politics, not technical complexity. Our objective scoring model and stakeholder-validated assessments provide air cover for difficult decisions. When someone says 'you can't touch my system,' we can show exactly how it scored and why, based on feedback from their own colleagues."

### 9. "We need to move faster than 8 weeks"

**Answer**: "We can accelerate to 6 weeks for focused scopes, but we don't recommend cutting the stakeholder assessment phase. The qualitative insights are what make recommendations stick. Skipping them is why previous rationalization efforts failed."

### 10. "Can't we just use a tool and do this ourselves?"

**Answer**: "Tools are important, but they're only 20% of the solution. The real value is in the methodology, the assessment framework, the stakeholder facilitation, and the expertise to interpret findings. We provide all of that, plus we've done this dozens of times and know where the opportunities hide."

### 11. "What if our data quality is poor?"

**Answer**: "It always is. We expect it and plan for it. Our discovery process includes data quality assessment in week 2, giving us time to identify gaps and find alternative sources. We triangulate data from multiple systems and use stakeholder interviews to fill in missing pieces."

### 12. "We're worried about business disruption"

**Answer**: "We're extremely careful about business impact. Our recommendations include detailed migration approaches, data handling plans, and risk mitigations. We identify 'quick win' retirements—truly unused applications—first, so you can build confidence before tackling more complex consolidations."

### 13. "How do you handle vendor relationships?"

**Answer**: "We don't make vendor-specific recommendations without client input. We identify consolidation opportunities and replacement scenarios, but vendor selection is always your decision. We can provide evaluation criteria and support vendor negotiations if helpful."

### 14. "What's different about your approach?"

**Answer**: "Three things: First, we combine automated discovery with structured stakeholder assessments—you get both hard data and soft insights. Second, we use a weighted, configurable scoring model that fits your priorities, not a one-size-fits-all framework. Third, we deliver in weeks, not months, so you can act while the data is still fresh."

## Use Case Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Post-Merger Integration

**Situation**: Company acquired a competitor and now has overlapping systems across both organizations.

**Our Approach**:
- Rapid inventory of both portfolios (2 weeks)
- Identify functional overlaps and consolidation candidates
- Assess which system is "best of breed" for each function
- Create integration roadmap with timeline and savings

**Outcome**: Clear consolidation plan, 40% reduction in combined portfolio, $20M savings over 3 years.

### Scenario 2: Cloud Migration Preparation

**Situation**: Enterprise committed to cloud-first but doesn't know what to migrate first or what to retire.

**Our Approach**:
- Complete portfolio assessment with cloud-readiness factors
- Score applications on migration complexity and business value
- Identify candidates for retire, rehost, replatform, refactor
- Create wave-based migration plan

**Outcome**: 30% of applications retired before migration, remaining apps sequenced by value and complexity, avoided $5M in unnecessary migration costs.

### Scenario 3: Cost Reduction Mandate

**Situation**: CFO mandated 20% IT cost reduction but CIO doesn't know where to cut without damaging business.

**Our Approach**:
- Full TCO analysis including hidden costs
- Identify low-value, high-cost applications
- Find redundant and underutilized licenses
- Model savings scenarios with business impact

**Outcome**: Identified $8M in savings opportunities with minimal business impact, prioritized roadmap for CFO presentation.

### Scenario 4: Technical Debt Remediation

**Situation**: Legacy applications creating security vulnerabilities and blocking new initiatives.

**Our Approach**:
- Assess technical health and security risk
- Map integration dependencies
- Identify modernization vs. replacement decisions
- Create prioritized remediation plan

**Outcome**: Critical vulnerabilities addressed in first wave, legacy burden reduced 35% over 18 months, development velocity increased 20%.
